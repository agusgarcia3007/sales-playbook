# Seller Psychology & Mindset

## The Inner Game (Brian Tracy)

The fundamental premise: **"Selling is an inner game."**

**Seller Self-Concept (Self-Concept Theory):**

1. **Self-Ideal** -- Who you aspire to be
2. **Self-Image** -- How you see yourself right now
3. **Self-Esteem** -- The alignment between the two

**The Financial Thermostat:** You'll never earn more than 10% above or below your self-concept level.

**Practical application:**
- Repeat affirmations before every call block
- Visualize yourself closing deals at an elite level
- Associate with top performers
- Replace negative self-talk with confidence statements

## The Three Tens of Certainty (Jordan Belfort)

The prospect won't buy until they have level 10 certainty on three scales:

1. **Certainty in the Product** -- Your product is the best option
2. **Certainty in You** -- They trust you as a person
3. **Certainty in the Company** -- They trust your organization

## State Management (Jordan Belfort)

Before any sales interaction, anchor your emotional state:
- Remember a peak performance moment
- Relive it vividly
- Anchor it to a physical gesture
- Activate that state before every call block

**Never call when you're flat. Activate your state first.**

## The 10X Rule (Grant Cardone)

- Set goals 10 times bigger than what you think you want
- Take 10 times more action than you think is necessary
- The 4 levels of action: Do Nothing, Retreat, Normal Action, **Massive Action**
- Only Massive Action produces extraordinary results

## Buoyancy - Resilience Against Rejection (Daniel Pink)

- **Before calling:** Use interrogative self-talk ("Can I make this a great conversation?")
- **During:** Maintain 3:1 ratio of positive to negative emotions
- **After rejection:** Frame as temporary, specific, and external

## Modern Sales Model (Brian Tracy - 40/30/20/10)

| Phase | % of Effort | Activity |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| 40% | Build Trust | Rapport, credibility, relationship |
| 30% | Identify Needs | Ask, listen, understand real problems |
| 20% | Present Solutions | Show how your product solves their specific problem |
| 10% | Confirm and Close | Ask for the decision and complete the sale |

## The Two Buying Motivations

- **Desire for gain** (motivational power: 1.0)
- **Fear of loss** (motivational power: 2.5)

People are 2.5x more motivated by the fear of losing something than by the desire to gain it.

## WII-FM (Brian Tracy)

> "The prospect doesn't care what your product is. They only care what your product will do for them. Every customer's favorite radio station is WII-FM: 'What's In It For Me?'"

## The Golden Triangle (Brian Tracy)

The three activities that represent 90% of a salesperson's value:

1. **Prospect** -- Find qualified people
2. **Present** -- Show your solution to their problem
3. **Close** -- Ask for the decision

**Rule:** Spend 80%+ of your day on these three activities.
