# Closing Techniques & Follow-Up

## CLOSING TECHNIQUES

### Assumptive Close
Act as if the sale is already decided. Skip "Do you want to buy?" and go straight to implementation.
> "Perfect. So the next step would be to set up your account and schedule onboarding. Does Monday or Wednesday work better?"

### Summary Close
Restate all agreed-upon value points before asking for commitment.
> "So: you need [requirement 1], [requirement 2], and [requirement 3]. We saw that [Product] does exactly that and saves you $X/month. The only step left is..."

### Puppy Dog Close (Trial Close)
Let the product sell itself through experience.
> "I understand it's an important decision. How about we give you access for 14 days, no credit card, and your team tests it? If it works, we continue. If not, no commitment."

### Alternative Close
Give two options -- both result in a sale.
> "We have two plans: the Pro with up to 500 users, or the Business with unlimited users and priority support. Which fits better?"

### 1-10 Close
Measure commitment level and address the gap.
> "On a scale of 1 to 10, where 10 is 'ready to start today', where do you feel?"
> If they say 7: "What would need to happen to take you from 7 to 10?"

### Sharp Angle Close
When they ask for a concession, use it as closing leverage.
> Prospect: "Could you include personalized onboarding?"
> You: "If I include onboarding, can we start this week?"

### Next Step Close
The softest close. Simply outline what happens next.
> "Next steps: first I send you access, which takes 5 minutes to set up. Then we schedule your onboarding. By Monday your account is live. Sound good?"

### Ben Franklin Close
The prospect draws a line on a page. Lists reasons to buy on one side and reasons not to on the other. Help them fill the "for" column.

### Doorknob Close
When you're leaving, turn and say: "Oh, by the way..." and introduce one last compelling point when defenses are down.

### Invitational Close (Brian Tracy)
Simply invite: "Why not give it a try?" or "Which package would you like?"

### Cardinal Rule of Closing (Brian Tracy)
> **"The only pressure allowed in a professional sales presentation is the pressure of silence after the closing question."**

Ask. Then SHUT UP. The first person to speak loses.

### TDPPR Formula for Post-Meeting Follow-Up (Brian Tracy)
Never leave a meeting without scheduling the next one:
- **T** -- Time
- **D** -- Date
- **P** -- Place/platform
- **P** -- Participants
- **R** -- Reason for the meeting

## FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE

### Key Statistic
Campaigns with 4-7 emails achieve 27% reply rate -- 3x more than 1-3 email sequences.

### 30-Day Sequence

| Day | Channel | Content |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Day 0 | WhatsApp or Email | First contact |
| Day 3 | Email | Quick value add: "Following up on my previous message... [data/case study]" |
| Day 7 | WhatsApp | Short message: "Here's a case study from [similar company] that switched" |
| Day 14 | Email | PAS email with economic loss data |
| Day 21 | WhatsApp | "I don't want to be pushy, but I think this is relevant for you. If you're not interested just let me know and I'll stop" |
| Day 30 | Email | **Breakup email** |

### The Breakup Email

Generates the highest response rate of the entire sequence through loss aversion.

```
Subject: Should I close your file?

Hi [Name],

I've written to you a couple of times and haven't heard back,
which usually means one of three things:

1. You're interested but it's not a good time - happy to
   reach out in a few months
2. You already solved it another way - great, I'll stop
   writing
3. It slipped through - no problem, reply "interested"
   and I'll send the details

I don't want to be the one filling your inbox. Let me know which one.

[Your name]
```

### Timing Rules
- **Day of week:** First email Tuesday or Wednesday. Follow-up Thursday or next Monday/Tuesday
- **Time:** 8-10 AM in the prospect's timezone
- **SMB:** 5-8 touchpoints in 30 days
- **Enterprise:** 10-18 touchpoints in 30-60 days
- **Multi-channel:** Limit emails to 50% of total touches. Mix WhatsApp, calls, voice notes
