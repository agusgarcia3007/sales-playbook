# Persuasion Psychology

## The 7 Principles of Persuasion (Robert Cialdini)

### Reciprocity
Give something first, before asking. The gift must be personalized, unexpected, and meaningful. A 2-minute Loom video analyzing their specific funnel is powerful.

**Application:** "I put together an analysis of how [their desired outcome] would look -- no strings attached."

### Commitment & Consistency
Start with small "yeses":
- "Is it okay if I ask you a few quick questions?" (Yes)
- "This is a real problem for you, right?" (Yes)
- "If a solution existed that solved this, would you consider it?" (Yes)

Each "yes" makes the final "yes" more likely.

### Social Proof
**Types (from most to least persuasive in B2B):**
1. **Peer social proof** -- Companies of similar size, industry, and stage
2. **Aspirational** -- Companies the prospect admires
3. **Quantitative** -- Raw numbers signaling momentum
4. **Expert** -- Endorsements from industry authorities
5. **Results-based** -- Specific results, not just logos

**Application:** "We work with 200+ companies in [industry], including [recognizable name]. After implementing, [Company X] saw a 34% increase in retention in 90 days."

### Authority
Establish credibility early but subtly. "Having worked with 50+ [industry] businesses on this exact problem..."

### Liking
Find genuine common ground. Give specific, real compliments. "Let's discover together if this makes sense for you."

### Scarcity
Only real scarcity: "We only onboard 10 new accounts per month because each client gets personalized onboarding -- there are 3 spots left."

### Unity
"As fellow [shared identity]..." / "We're [shared identity] like you, that's why we built this."

## Tactical Empathy (Chris Voss)

Understanding the feelings AND the mindset behind those feelings.

**Before responding to any objection, PAUSE and acknowledge:**
> "Seems like this isn't easy for you..."
> "Sounds like you've had bad experiences with vendors before."

## Mirroring (Chris Voss)

Repeat the last 1-3 words the other person said. Use a calm, curious tone. Then SILENCE.

> Prospect: "We don't have budget for another tool."
> You: "Another tool?" [silence]
> Prospect: "Well, we do have it, but it's allocated to marketing..."

## Labeling (Chris Voss)

Name the emotion or perspective. Start with "It seems like..." / "It sounds like..."

> "It seems like the experience with your current platform has left you frustrated..."

**After labeling, SILENCE.** Let the label do the work.

## Calibrated Questions (Chris Voss)

Open-ended "How" and "What" questions that give the illusion of control to the other person:

| Situation | Calibrated Question |
|---|---|
| They say it's expensive | "How do I make this work for you?" |
| Need approval | "What's the approval process on your end?" |
| They're stuck | "What's holding you back from moving forward?" |
| After agreement | "How will we know this is working?" |
| Want a discount | "How am I supposed to do that?" (gently) |
| Discover decision | "What would you need to see to feel comfortable moving forward?" |
| Identify stakeholders | "Who else would need to weigh in on this?" |
| Understand timeline | "What happens if this isn't resolved by [date]?" |

## Accusation Audit (Chris Voss)

Before they can express objections, name every negative thing they might be thinking:

> "Look, you're probably thinking: 'Another salesperson who's going to promise the moon and not deliver.' And I completely understand why you'd think that. I'm not here for that."

## The Ben Franklin Effect

People who do you a favor end up liking you more.

**Application:** "I'm researching how [industry] professionals handle [relevant activity]. I saw you have experience. Can I ask you a quick question?"

## Sales Storytelling

**Success Story Framework:**
1. **Character** (a client like the prospect): "A fitness coach in [location]..."
2. **Conflict** (a problem they can identify with): "...was paying 20% commission on [platform]..."
3. **Journey** (the experience of working with you): "...switched to [solution] in one week..."
4. **Resolution** (measurable result): "...and in 90 days saved $3,600 in commissions."
5. **Moral** (transferable insight): "Eliminating commissions completely changes the economics of [their business type]."

**Rule:** Make the client the hero, not your product.

## Loss Aversion & FOMO

Losses feel 2x more intense than equivalent gains (Kahneman & Tversky).

**Instead of:** "You'll gain 20% more revenue" --> **Say:** "You're leaving 20% of revenue on the table every month"
**Instead of:** "You'll save 10 hours a week" --> **Say:** "You're losing 10 hours a week to manual processes"

## Sell the Transformation, Not the Product

**Bad approach:** "Our platform automates course delivery with video hosting and payment gateway."

**Good approach:** "What would your month look like if every sale was 100% yours? Companies like [X] stopped giving away $1,000/month in commissions."

## The Trust Formula

> Trust = (Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy) / Self-Orientation

**Techniques:**
1. Name-drop with context: "[Name] suggested I reach out to you"
2. Lead with relevant insight, not with a pitch
3. Acknowledge their time: "I know this is unsolicited, so I'll get to the point"
4. Use THEIR language
5. Be specific, not generic
6. Pattern interrupt: "This is a cold call -- want to hang up or give me 30 seconds?"

## The 11 Emotional Buying Drivers (Brian Tracy)

Money, Security, Being liked, Status and prestige, Health and fitness, Recognition and praise, Power and influence, Leading the field, Love and companionship, Personal growth, Personal transformation.

## The 6 Buyer Types (Brian Tracy)

| Type | Characteristics | How to Sell |
|---|---|---|
| **Apathetic** | No interest, no motivation | Avoid -- don't waste time |
| **Self-Actualizing** | Knows exactly what they want | Be direct, efficient |
| **Analytical** | Wants data, ROI, proof | Provide detailed information, be methodical |
| **Relational** | Relationship-driven, cautious | Build trust, give reassurance, be patient |
| **Driver** | Direct, results-focused, impatient | Be fast, results-oriented, get to the point |
| **Socializer** | Achievement-oriented, wants recognition | Acknowledge their success, show status benefits |
